This guide covers how to integrate your services with our Prometheus setup. For advanced PromQL querying or general concepts, refer to the [official Prometheus documentation](https://prometheus.io/docs/introduction/overview/).

## Ensuring Your Service is Scraped

We utilize standard Kubernetes annotations to instruct Prometheus on what to scrape.  
To ensure Prometheus picks up your metrics, your **pods** (not deployments) must reside in one of the monitored namespaces and include these annotations:
* `prometheus.io/scrape`
* `prometheus.io/port`
* `prometheus.io/path`

## Managing Prometheus Alerts

Our Prometheus alert rules are defined centrally as code. These rules use PromQL expressions to evaluate conditions over time. 

```yaml title="Alerts Config"
alerting_rules.yml:
  groups:
    - name: blackbox_alerts
      rules:
        - alert: CertExpiration
          expr: ((probe_ssl_earliest_cert_expiry{job="blackbox"} - time()) / 3600 / 24 < 30)
          for: 90s
          labels:
            severity: 'critical'
          annotations:
            description: "A certificate ({{ $labels.instance }}) is about to expire in 30 days!"
            summary: "A certificate ({{ $labels.instance }}) is about to expire in 30 days!"
```

To add, update, or remove an alert, create a Pull Request [here](https://github.com/MapColonies/helm-charts/blob/master/infra/monitoring/values/alerts-values.yaml) 
with your new or modified alert rule and notify Infra team to review and apply the upgrade.
