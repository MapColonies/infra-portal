This guide covers how to ensure your application logs are properly collected and sent to Loki. 
For advanced collector configurations, refer to the [official Grafana Alloy documentation](https://grafana.com/docs/alloy/latest/).

## Method 1: Pushing Logs Directly (Recommended)

The standard and recommended way to get your application logs into our observability stack is by configuring your service to push them directly to our internal Alloy endpoint.

* **Internal Endpoint:** `http://infra-alloy`

Make sure you configure the appropriate port:
* 4317 - grpc
* 4318 - http

## Method 2: Scraping via Pod Labels

This method should only be used for 3rd-party services or legacy applications that do not have the native ability to push logs directly over the network.

To tell Alloy to scrape these pods, add the following custom label to the deployment template:

**Label Key:** `mapcolonies.io/alloy-api-logs`  
**Label Value:** `"true"`

```yaml title="Deployment Template"
kind: Deployment
apiVersion: apps/v1
metadata:
  # Deployment labels - Not here ❌
  labels:
    app.kubernetes.io/name: SOME_NAME
spec:
  template:
    metadata:
      # Pod labels - Here ✅
      labels:
        mapcolonies.io/alloy-api-logs: 'true'
```
