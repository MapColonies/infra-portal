# Prometheus

You can access Libot's Prometheus at [https://prometheus.mapcolonies.net](https://prometheus.mapcolonies.net).

Prometheus is our open-source monitoring and alerting system, designed to collect and store metrics as time-series data.  
It serves as the core metric engine of our observability stack.

![prometheus homescreen](/img/monitoring/prometheus/prometheus-homescreen.png)

## Service-Level Scraping Architecture

To improve accuracy and reduce load, our Prometheus is configured to support scraping metrics at the **Service** level, rather than just the Pod level. 

When scraping only at the pod level, metrics representing global cluster states (like a shared cache or global active connections) are duplicated 
for every replica you have running. By scraping the Service endpoint instead, Prometheus queries the metric just once, providing accurate, 
deduplicated cluster-wide metrics without artificial inflation.

## Alerting Engine

Prometheus constantly evaluates defined alert rules against the collected metrics. When conditions are met (such as high CPU usage, certificate expiration, or service downtime), Prometheus fires these alerts and forwards them to Alertmanager for routing and notification. 

![prometheus alerts](/img/monitoring/prometheus/prometheus-alerts.png)

## Exporters

Prometheus scrapes data from our own applications as well as a variety of specialized exporters. Documentation for specific integrations (such as the Blackbox Exporter, Postgres Exporter, and Kube State Metrics) can be found in our dedicated **[Exporters Directory](./exporters/README.mdx)**.
